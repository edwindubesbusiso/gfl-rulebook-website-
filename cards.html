Discipline
Card System

The GFL adopts a partial standard of the FIFA card system. Referees are required to apply the following consistently and fairly for both teams throughout every match.

Yellow Card — Caution

A yellow card is a formal warning issued for unsporting behaviour. Offences include but are not limited to:

  • Unsporting fouls or tackles
  • Dissent by word or action toward the referee
  • Persistent infringement of the rules
  • Deliberately delaying the restart of play
  • Entering or re-entering the field without the referee's permission
Consequence: Two yellow cards in a single match result in an automatic red card. Accumulation of yellow cards across multiple matches may lead to a suspension as determined by the Disciplinary Team.
Red Card — Ejection

A red card is shown for serious foul play or violent conduct. The player is ejected from the match immediately. Offences include but are not limited to:

  • Serious foul play or violent conduct
  • Spitting at any person on or off the pitch
  • Denying an obvious goal-scoring opportunity via handball or foul
  • Using offensive, insulting, or abusive language and/or gestures
  • Receiving a second yellow card in the same match
Consequence: The player is immediately ejected. Their team must play with one fewer player for the remainder of the match. The player is automatically suspended for the next league match. Further disciplinary action may follow after a Disciplinary Team review.

Cumulative Sanctions

Yellow Card Accumulation: Multiple yellow cards received across different matches over the course of the season may trigger a suspension. The Disciplinary Team will determine the length and terms of any suspension.
Season Red Card Ban: If a player receives 2 red cards in a single season, they are banned from the league entirely. This ban is subject to review by the Disciplinary Team.
Disciplinary Review: All red card incidents are automatically reviewed by the independent Disciplinary Team. Additional sanctions — including extended bans or fines — may be applied after review of the match report and any available evidence.
Appeals: Players or teams may submit a formal appeal against any disciplinary decision to the Disciplinary Team. Appeals must be submitted within a defined timeline as set by the Disciplinary Team. Their decision on appeal is final.
