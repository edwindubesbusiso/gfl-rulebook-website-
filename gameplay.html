On the Pitch
Gameplay Rules

Pre-Match Reminders

Managers and Assistant Managers are responsible for communicating these reminders to their players before every match.

⏱ Early Arrival: All players must arrive at the venue at least 20 minutes before kick-off to allow for registration, warm-up, and a team talk.
🤝 Greet With Respect: Greet the opposing team, match officials, and league organizers with a spirit of friendly competition — as we are all witnesses.
💍 No Jewellery: All jewellery must be fully removed before stepping onto the field. Jewellery can cause injury to yourself or others during play.

Safety & Valuables

The safety of participants and their property is a shared responsibility. Please take note of the following precautions.

📱 Secure Your Valuables: Mobile phones, watches, and wallets should be left with trusted fans or secured in a locked car and kept out of sight, as some venues are open to non-witnesses.
🚗 Vehicle Parking: All vehicles must be parked only in designated parking areas. Do not block emergency exits, access roads, or other vehicles. Ask the brother who booked the venue for parking guidance.
🔒 Property Disclaimer: The GFL, its organizers, and the venue are not responsible for any loss, theft, or damage to personal property — including vehicles and their contents. This is covered in the liability waiver.

Referee Duties & Reminders

The referee's decision on the field of play is final. Referees are reminded of their key duties to ensure consistency and safety.

🚨 Whistle for Safety: The referee holds ultimate authority to stop, suspend, or abandon a match for any safety concerns — including severe weather, poor lighting, or spectator behaviour.
🩺 Player Welfare: Be vigilant for signs of player injury. Stop play immediately for any suspected head injury. A player with an open bleeding wound must not remain on the field of play.
⚖️ Consistency: Apply the rules and the card system consistently and fairly for both teams throughout the entire match.
🗣️ Game Management: Communicate clearly with players. Explain decisions when appropriate to help de-escalate tension and maintain a positive atmosphere.
📝 Post-Match Report: Submit a brief, accurate match report to the league organizers immediately after the game. The report must note any incidents, all goalscorers, and any cards issued.
